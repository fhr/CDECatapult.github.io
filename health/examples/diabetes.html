Studies

    Over 2.4 million people in England or about 5.5% have diabetes. On average £24.45 is spent per diabetes patient per month on prescriptions to treat their condition.

    This site takes data from sources like the Health and Social Care Information Centre (HSCIC) and shows how it can be turned into visualisations and analysis to answer specific questions for your organisation. This is data science example, which shows how to take a small number of datasets including data about GP prescriptions and diabetes prevalence to show the extent of diabetes treatment in the UK.

    Spend per diabetes patient

    This chart shows how much money is spent on prescriptions for diabetes patients per month. The data is at the Clinical Commissioning Group (CCG) level, which is the way GP Surgeries have organised in England since 1 April 2013.

    The amount spent per diabetes patient ranges from roughly £18 to £31 per month.

    Diabetes Prevalence

    This chart shows what percentage of people in a CCG have diabetes. About 5.5% of people in England have diabetes, but the prevalence in a particular area can be from 3% to over 9%.

    Clicking on a CCG in the map will show the GP Surgeries with the highest prevalence of diabetes in that CCG below.
