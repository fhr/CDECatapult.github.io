ADHD

This site takes data from sources like the Health and Social Care Information Centre (HSCIC) and shows how it can be turned into visualisations and analysis to answer specific questions for your organisation. This is the ALPHA version of the site

Prescriptions per head per GP

This chart shows the raw numbers for each GP, taken over a 12 month period.

Totalled up are the number of prescriptions handed out per patient in each practice

There is a clear outlier, which turns out to be a GP serving only a very small number of patients - for the rest of this study they will be removed so as not to skew the results.

Understanding the distribution

Mapping the prescriptions per head into a histogram, we can see that the majority of GPs are below average in the number of prescriptions, and a small number of GPs skew the results by being consistently above average.

This is actually an example of a lognormal distribution, which will impact how we calculate the variance when comparing different CCGs.

Prescription variance across the UK

We can map the variance of this data across England, grouping the GP data by the CCG they reside in, to see if there are any particular areas to investigate further.

We might perhaps expect perhaps that the areas of high concentration are because of an outlying GP (specialists)

Hover over a CCG in the map above to see the breakdown in that area.

Summary

The areas of high concentration contain GPs that consistently prescribe ADHD drugs at a rate much higher than that of the national average, and the areas of low concentration contain GPs that consistently prescribe ADHD drugs at a rate much lower than that of the national average

What is interesting, is that in bordering CCGs (such as the Wirral and Liverpool), is the stark divide in prescribing practises between the two.

More work would need to be done to determine the reason for this, as it is unlikely to be anything as simple as "There is a higher prevalence of ADHD in those areas. It would be interesting to look at factors such as the birth-rates, economic factors and similar too.
